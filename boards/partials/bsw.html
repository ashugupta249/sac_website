Board for Student Welfare

About

Board for Student Welfare, is a committee designed to look after the welfare of the entire student population of IIT Delhi. The aim of the committee is two-fold. First, to solve pressing social and mental-health issues faced by students during their stay at IIT. Second, to provide opportunities which help students gain perspective about themselves and their career. It consists of a dedicated team of students, across years, committed to carry out these responsibilities. Breaking down the BSW, it has two core branches - operations and mentorship. The Operations team is involved in organising Speranza(annual freshers’ fest), essential camps for books, cycles and blankets, conducting informative sessions for foreign internship, exchange and career counseling among other welfare activities throughout the year. The Mentorship team has succeeded in setting up a strong and diverse program, which includes language, academic and alumni mentorship being provided to students by experts. In addition to these, the innovative initiatives undertaken by the BSW such as “YourDOST”, “AlmaConnect” and “BSWLoans” has helped ease the life of students at IIT and we believe, has also transformed them into confident and self-assured individuals.
